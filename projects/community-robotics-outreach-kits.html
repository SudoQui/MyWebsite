Community Robotics Outreach Kits

Portable robotics learning kits designed for community workshops, enabling practical STEM education with reusable hardware and guided activities.

Problem or Motivation

Many students are interested in robotics but lack hands-on exposure. The goal was to make practical robotics learning accessible through affordable and repeatable kit-based workshops.

What I Built

  • Modular hardware kit with simple assembly and troubleshooting guides.
  • Step-by-step curriculum covering sensing, control, and autonomous behavior basics.
  • Reusable workshop infrastructure for mentors and facilitators.

Tools and Technologies Used

  • Arduino-based control boards and sensor kits
  • CAD for enclosure and mount design
  • Python and embedded exercises for introductory robotics workflows

My Role and Contributions

  • Led kit architecture, activity sequencing, and documentation approach.
  • Facilitated workshops and refined content based on participant feedback.
  • Coordinated logistics and quality checks for repeat events.

Challenges and Lessons Learned

  • Hardware durability needed improvements for repeated transport and setup.
  • Clear instructions greatly reduced onboarding time for beginners.
  • Community impact grows when engineering is taught through building.
